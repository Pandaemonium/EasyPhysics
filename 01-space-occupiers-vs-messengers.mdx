# The Foundation of Matter

Every bit of matter you interact with is governed by a fundamental dichotomy: there are particles that refuse to overlap, and particles that easily overlap.

## The Brick and the Laser

Imagine trying to push your hand through a solid wooden table. You can't. The particles making up the table refuse to share the same physical space and quantum state as the particles in your hand. We call these particles <Term intuitive="Space-Occupiers" jargon="Fermions" definition="Particles governed by the Pauli Exclusion Principle that provide the physical volume and structure of matter." />. Because they resist overlapping, they give structural rigidity to everything you can touch.

On the other hand, consider two laser beams crossing paths. They pass right through each other without crashing. The particles making up light do not obey the rule of exclusion; they are perfectly happy occupying the exact same space. We call these particles <Term intuitive="Force-Carriers" jargon="Bosons" definition="Particles with integer spin that mediate interactions and transfer momentum/energy between space-occupiers." />. They act as messengers, transferring energy and fundamental forces between the Space-Occupiers.
