# The Pixelation of the Universe

We often think of the world in two distinct categories: solid objects (like a baseball) and continuous waves (like ripples in a pond). But at the fundamental level of reality, this distinction breaks down completely.

## Zooming In

Think about a digital photograph. From a few feet away, the image looks perfectly smooth, with continuous gradients of color. However, if you zoom in close enough, you realize the image is constructed from thousands of tiny, distinct, indivisible squares—pixels. 

The energy in the universe works the exact same way. Energy and fields appear continuous to our macroscopic eyes, but when you look closely, they exist in distinct "chunks" or packets. This is known as <Term intuitive="Granular Wave Behavior" jargon="Wave-Particle Duality" definition="The concept that every fundamental entity exhibits properties of both continuous waves and discrete particles." />.

A particle isn't a tiny, solid billiard ball. It is simply a localized ripple—a tiny, indivisible excitation within an underlying universal field.
