# Coherence

## 1. Scope
This article explains **coherence** as the property of waves that allows them to maintain a **constant phase relationship** over time and space. It covers:
- Definitions of temporal and spatial coherence
- Examples (lasers, radio waves)
- How coherence enables interference patterns
- Applications in optics and wave physics

## 2. Out of Scope
This article does **not** explain:
- Quantum coherence (e.g., entangled states)
- Advanced applications like quantum computing or gravitational wave detection
- Mathematical formalisms beyond basic wave equations

## 3. Prerequisite Concepts
- Basic wave properties (amplitude, frequency, wavelength)
- Interference (constructive/destructive)
- Phase of a wave (as a "position in the cycle")

## 4. Core Intuition
Coherence is about **waves staying in sync**. When two waves are coherent, their peaks and troughs align consistently, creating stable interference patterns. Incoherent waves "lose sync" randomly, leading to unpredictable patterns. Think of it like two metronomes: coherent waves are like metronomes ticking in perfect unison, while incoherent waves are like metronomes with random timing.

## 5. Suggested Descriptive Names
- **Phase-locked waves** {coherent waves}
- **Stable phase relationship** {coherence}
- **Temporal coherence** {coherence in time}
- **Spatial coherence** {coherence in space}
- **Phase-locked source** {coherent source}

## 6. Common Misconceptions to Avoid
- ❌ "Coherence means high intensity": No—coherence is about phase, not brightness.
- ❌ "All waves are coherent": Most natural waves (e.g., sunlight) are incoherent.
- ❌ "Coherence is about frequency": Coherence involves both frequency and phase stability.

## 7. Section-by-Section Outline
### 1. Waves and Phase
- What is a wave's "phase"? (e.g., "position in the cycle")
- How phase differences create interference

### 2. What is Coherence?
- Definition: Consistent phase relationship over time/space
- Contrast with incoherence (random phase shifts)

### 3. Types of Coherence
- **Temporal coherence**: How long a wave stays coherent (e.g., laser light)
- **Spatial coherence**: How consistent the phase is across space (e.g., parallel wavefronts)

### 4. Examples of Coherent Waves
- Lasers: How they produce coherent light
- Radio waves: How they maintain coherence over long distances

### 5. How to Achieve Coherence
- Methods: Using single-source emitters, optical cavities, or phase-locked oscillators

### 6. Why Coherence Matters
- Enables interference patterns (e.g., Young's double-slit)
- Applications: Holography, interferometry, fiber optics

## 9. Optional Minimal Math
- **Phase difference equation**:  
  Δφ = 2πΔx/λ (for spatial coherence)  
  *Explanation*: A constant phase difference (Δφ) ensures stable interference.  
- **Coherence time and length**:  
  τ = Δt (time over which phase stays stable)  
  L = cτ (coherence length: how far the wave remains coherent)  
  *Explanation*: These quantify how "long" or "far" coherence persists.
