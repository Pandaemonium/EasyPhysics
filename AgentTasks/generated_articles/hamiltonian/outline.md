# Hamiltonian

## 1. Scope  
This article explains the **Hamiltonian** as the central rulebook for how physical systems evolve over time. It covers:  
- The Hamiltonian as the total energy of a system in classical mechanics.  
- The Hamiltonian as the operator governing quantum systems’ time evolution.  
- How the Hamiltonian connects forces, energy, and motion in both frameworks.  

---

This article will **not** cover:  
- Advanced topics like quantum field theory or relativistic Hamiltonians.

## 3. Prerequisite Concepts  
- **Energy** (kinetic + potential).  
- **Forces** and how they relate to motion.  
- **Wavefunctions** and quantum states (for quantum mechanics).  
- **Operators** and how they act on states.  
- **Time evolution** of systems.  

---

## 4. Core Intuition  
The **Hamiltonian** is the **energy governor** {Hamiltonian} of a system. It encodes:  
- **What the system’s total energy is** (classical: kinetic + potential; quantum: operator).  
- **How the system changes over time** (via equations of motion or probability rules).  
- **The balance between forces and motion** (classical) or **the rules for quantum probabilities** (quantum).  

**Analogy**: Imagine the Hamiltonian as a recipe for how a system “bakes” itself over time—ingredients (energy) and steps (dynamics).

## 6. Common Misconceptions to Avoid  
- ❌ **"The Hamiltonian is just a formula for energy."**  
  → In quantum mechanics, it’s an **operator** that acts on states, not just a number.  
- ❌ **"Hamiltonian and Lagrangian are the same."**  
  → They’re different tools for describing dynamics; the Hamiltonian focuses on energy, while the Lagrangian focuses on differences in energy.  
- ❌ **"The Hamiltonian only applies to particles."**  
  → It applies to fields, waves, and even abstract systems (e.g., quantum states).

## 9. Optional Minimal Math  
### **Classical Mechanics**  
- **Hamilton’s equations**:  
  $$
  \frac{dp}{dt} = -\frac{\partial H}{\partial q}, \quad \frac{dq}{dt} = \frac{\partial H}{\partial p}
  $$  
  → *The Hamiltonian tells how momentum and position change over time.*  

### **Quantum Mechanics**  
- **Schrödinger equation**:  
  $$
  i\hbar \frac{\partial \psi}{\partial t} = \hat{H} \psi
  $$  
  → *The Hamiltonian operator {operator} dictates how the wavefunction {wavefunction} evolves.*
