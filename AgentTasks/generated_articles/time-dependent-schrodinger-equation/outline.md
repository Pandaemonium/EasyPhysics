# Time-dependent Schrödinger equation

---

## 1. Scope  
This article explains the **time-dependent Schrödinger equation (TDSE)** as the fundamental rule governing how quantum systems evolve over time. It describes how the probability wave (wavefunction) of a particle changes with time, incorporating energy, forces, and quantum rules. The article focuses on its role in quantum mechanics, its mathematical form, and its implications for understanding time evolution in quantum systems.

## 3. Prerequisite Concepts  
- **Wavefunction** {ψ}: A mathematical description of a quantum system’s state.  
- **Probability amplitude**: The wavefunction’s modulus squared gives probabilities.  
- **Hamiltonian operator** {H}: Represents total energy (kinetic + potential) in quantum systems.  
- **Complex numbers**: Understanding of imaginary unit *i* and complex exponentials.  
- **Time-independent Schrödinger equation (TISE)** {stationary states}: A simpler version for systems with fixed energy.  

---

## 4. Core Intuition  
The **TDSE** is the "quantum clock" that tells us how a system’s probability wave evolves over time. It combines:  
- **Energy** (via the Hamiltonian)  
- **Time** (via the imaginary unit *i*)  
- **Wave-like behavior** (via complex numbers)  

Think of it as a recipe:  
> "The rate of change of the wavefunction is proportional to its energy, multiplied by a complex factor."  

This equation replaces classical Newtonian mechanics with a probabilistic, wave-based rule for time evolution.

## 6. Common Misconceptions to Avoid  
- ❌ **"The TDSE is just a time-dependent version of the TISE."**  
  → The TDSE is a separate equation; the TISE is a special case for systems with fixed energy.  
- ❌ **"The wavefunction is a physical wave."**  
  → The wavefunction is a mathematical tool for calculating probabilities, not a physical entity.  
- ❌ **"The TDSE predicts exact positions over time."**  
  → The TDSE describes probabilities, not deterministic paths.

### **2. The Equation Itself**  
- **Mathematical form**:  
  $ i\hbar \frac{\partial \psi}{\partial t} = \hat{H} \psi $  
  - Explain each symbol: *i*, *ħ*, *ψ*, *∂/∂t*, and *Ĥ*.  
- **Key components**:  
  - **Hamiltonian operator** {Ĥ}: Encodes energy (kinetic + potential).  
  - **Imaginary unit** *i*: Connects time evolution to probability.  

### **3. Intuition Behind the Equation**  
- **Energy-time relationship**: Higher energy means faster time evolution.  
- **Complex numbers**: Represent phase changes in probability waves.  
- **Wavefunction as a recipe**: How the equation "bakes" the wavefunction over time.
