# Variational Principle  

## The core idea  
Nature chooses paths or states that **extremize** a key quantity—like action in classical mechanics or the **expectation value of energy** in quantum systems. Think of it as a "smart choice" that balances competing factors, such as speed and energy cost. This rule applies across physics, from the motion of planets to the behavior of particles.  

## Why this matters  
The variational principle unifies seemingly different areas of physics. It explains why light bends, why objects fall, and why quantum systems settle into specific states. By focusing on "optimal" paths or states, it reveals a deep, universal structure in how nature operates.
