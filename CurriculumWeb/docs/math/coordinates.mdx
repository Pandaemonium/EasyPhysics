# Math Lens: Coordinates

This page is optional. The core idea is simple: <Term name="coordinate">coordinates</Term> are numbers we use to describe something. They are not the thing itself.

If a dot sits on a page, you might describe it with two numbers:

```text
x = 3
y = 2
```

Those numbers mean "3 units across" and "2 units up" from a chosen origin. Move the origin, rotate the axes, or choose different units, and the coordinate numbers can change even though the dot itself has not moved.

That distinction matters in physics. A <Term name="state">state</Term> may be written using coordinates, but the physical situation is not identical to one particular coordinate choice.

## The Smallest Useful Lesson

Coordinates answer the question:

```text
How do we label this situation so we can calculate with it?
```

They do not answer:

```text
Which labels are the universe secretly made of?
```

Later, relativity will lean hard on this distinction. Different observers can assign different coordinates to the same event, while agreeing about deeper quantities.
