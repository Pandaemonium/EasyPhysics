# What Is Physics Looking For?

If you drop a ball, turn on a lamp, heat a cup of water, or watch a planet move, many details change. The color of the table might not matter. The day of the week might not matter. The exact person doing the experiment might not matter.

<Term name="physics">Physics</Term> starts by asking a sharp question:

**What stays reliable while the situation changes?**

That is why physics is not just a warehouse of facts. It is a search for structure. A good physical idea lets you change the example and still recognize the same rule underneath.

A <Term name="system">system</Term> is the part of the world we are tracking. A <Term name="model">model</Term> is the useful description we choose for that system. The model is never the whole universe in full detail. It is the part of the description that helps us answer a question.

For a falling ball, the model might track height and speed. For a chess game, it might track the pieces on the board and whose turn it is. For weather, it might track temperature, pressure, humidity, and wind.

Different questions need different descriptions. Physics begins when we choose the description carefully enough that change becomes understandable.
