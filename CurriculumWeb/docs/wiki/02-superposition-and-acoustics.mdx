# Cosmic Chords

One of the most famously misunderstood concepts in physics is the idea that a particle can "be in multiple places at once." This sounds like magic, but it is actually a very natural, mechanical property of wave systems.

## The Guitar String Analogy

Think about plucking a thick string on a guitar or striking a snare drum. The string doesn't vibrate with just one perfectly clean shape. It vibrates with a fundamental tone, but also with dozens of smaller, overlapping harmonic frequencies at the exact same time. The structural tension of the string allows it to hold multiple vibrational states simultaneously.

This is exactly how <Term intuitive="Acoustic Overlap" jargon="Quantum Superposition" definition="The principle that quantum systems can exist in multiple potential vibrational states simultaneously until observed." /> works. Because particles are fundamentally waves, they can sustain multiple energetic states at once. The particle isn't physically split into two locations; its wave function is simply vibrating with a complex combination of possibilities until an interaction forces it to resolve into a single, clean "note."
