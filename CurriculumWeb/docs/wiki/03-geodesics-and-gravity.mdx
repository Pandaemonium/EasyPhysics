# Falling Through Curves

Gravity is often taught as an invisible, magnetic-like pull between two massive objects. This creates confusion when we try to understand *how* that pull magically travels across empty space. The intuitive truth is much simpler: **gravity is just geometry**.

## The Rubber Sheet Analogy

Imagine space and time are woven together into a single, flexible fabric. If you roll a marble across an empty, flat stretch of this fabric, it moves in a perfectly straight line. 

Now, place a heavy bowling ball in the middle. The fabric stretches and curves down around the heavy weight. If you roll the marble again, it won't travel in a straight line from your perspective. Instead, its path will curve inward toward the bowling ball. 

The bowling ball didn't "reach out and pull" the marble. The marble was simply following the <Term intuitive="straightest possible path" jargon="Geodesic" definition="The shortest or straightest possible path between two points in curved spacetime." /> along a surface that was no longer flat. 

Earth orbits the Sun not because of an invisible tether, but because the Sun severely bends the spacetime around it, and Earth is simply rolling along the natural curves of that geometry.
