# Conservation: The Memory of a Symmetry

A <Term name="conservation-law">conservation law</Term> says that some quantity stays fixed while the system changes.

That might sound like a magic promise. It is better to hear it as a clue: if something is conserved, there is usually a reason in the rulebook.

In more advanced language, <Term name="noether-theorem">Noether's theorem</Term> says that continuous symmetries of the action correspond to conserved quantities. You do not need the full math yet. The first-pass meaning is enough:

**When the rulebook ignores a smooth kind of change, a matching quantity is protected.**

## Conservation Does Not Mean Nothing Happens

A pendulum moves. Its height and speed keep trading. During that motion, energy can remain fixed in the ideal model.

Conservation means the bookkeeping total stays the same, not that every visible piece stays unchanged.
